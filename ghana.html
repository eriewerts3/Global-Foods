Appetizer - Kelewele

Ingredients

  • 4-6 ripe plantains
  • 1/4 onion cut into pieces
  • 1 tsp cayenne or chili pepper
  • 1 tbsp chopped ginger
  • 1-2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp bouillon powder
  • Your choice of oil for deep frying

Recipe

  1. Using a sharp knife, cut both ends off the plantain. Slit a shallow line down the long seam of the plantain, cut only as deep as the peel. Remove plantain peel by pulling it back.
  2. Cut plantains in diagonal pieces and set aside.
  3. Blend onions, ginger and garlic in a food processor with just enough water to facilitate blending.
  4. Add cayenne pepper, lemon juice, nutmeg, salt and bouillon. Set aside.
  5. AIn a large bowl, toss the plantain cubes and ginger spice mixture.
  6. Let it rest for about 10 to 20 minutes to absorb the flavor.
  7. Heat up a large skillet or cast iron with oil up to 1/2 inch - medium-high heat until hot but not smoking.
  8. Fry the plantain cubes in batches, turning once, until golden brown - about 5 minutes.
  9. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the fried plantains to pan lined with paper towels.
  10. Serve warm.
Main Course - Goat Light Soup w/ Fufu

Ingredients

  • 18 oz. diced goat or lamb
  • 6 whole tomatoes
  • 1 Scotch Bonnet chili pepper
  • 2 tsp bouillon powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger root, peeled and minced
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 2 onions
  • t tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt
  • 6 Garden Eggs (The African version
    of the eggplant - they are slightly smaller than American eggplants)

Recipe

  1. Place ginger, garlic, pepper, 1 onion, and 3 tomatoes in blender, season and blend to paste.
  2. Place the mix on the meat with a marinade for 10 minutes.
  3. Place the meat in a pan, cook gently for 10 minutes.
  4. Add tomato puree, cook for 10 minutes. Add 17 oz. of water and simmmer until meat is tender.
  5. Dice and add the remaining 3 tomatoes, diced eggplants, bouillon powder, and the remaining onion.
  6. Cook for 30 minutes.
  7. remove onions and tomatoes and pass it through the blender, add to soup again. Bring to simmer - season to taste.
  8. Serve Ghanaian Goat Light Soup with Fufu.
Cassava and Plantain Fufu Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cup un-ripened plantain
  • 1 lb. cassava
  • 480ml of water for blending
  • 80ml of water for steaming

Recipe

  1. Peel the plantain and cut into cubes. Peel and scrape the cassava, de-string it, and cut into cubes.
  2. Place the cassava and plantain into a blender. Add 350ml if you want firm fufu, and 450ml for a softer fufu.
  3. Process into a smooth paste.
  4. Heat the paste in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir constantly with a wooden spatula for 8-10 min to remove lumps.
  5. Add remaining 50ml of water to the mixture. Reduce heat to lowest setting, cover with lid and steam for another 8-10 min.
  6. Increase heat to medium and stir. The fufu might look too soft, but it will become firmer as it cools.
  7. Transfer the fufu into a bowl and sprinkle 1 pst of water on the surface to prevent a film from forming. Let cool completely.
  8. Shape fufu into a ball and serve with soup.
